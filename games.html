Titanfall 2: $30

Titanfall is a fast-paced first-person shooter set in a futuristic world where advanced technology and warfare collide. The game focuses on the conflict between large factions fighting for control of space colonies on the “Frontier,” a distant region of space. What makes it unique is the combination of agile human soldiers, known as Pilots, who can run on walls and move quickly through environments, and massive mechanized suits called Titans, which players can control for heavy combat. The gameplay blends speed, strategy, and large-scale battles, creating a dynamic experience where players seamlessly switch between infantry and mech warfare.

Fallout 4: $50

Fallout 4 is an open-world role-playing game set in a post-apocalyptic version of Boston, known as the Commonwealth, after a devastating nuclear war. You play as the “Sole Survivor,” emerging from an underground vault to search for your missing child while navigating a dangerous world filled with mutated creatures, hostile factions, and scattered survivors. The game blends exploration, combat, and decision-making, allowing players to shape the story through their choices, build and manage settlements, and customize weapons and armor in a richly detailed wasteland.

Team Fortress 2: $5

Team Fortress 2 is a team-based multiplayer shooter that focuses on objective-driven gameplay and distinct character roles. Players choose from a variety of classes, each with unique abilities and weapons, such as the fast-moving Scout or the defensive Engineer. The game centres around two rival teams competing in modes like capturing points or escorting payloads, with a strong emphasis on teamwork and strategy. Its cartoon-style graphics and humorous tone set it apart, creating a fun and chaotic experience while still rewarding skill and coordination.
